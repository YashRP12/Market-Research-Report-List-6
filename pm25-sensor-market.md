<p><p>The PM2.5 sensor market comprises two primary types: infrared sensors and laser sensors. Infrared sensors detect particulate matter by measuring the amount of light absorbed by fine particles, offering cost-effective solutions for less precision-demanding applications. In contrast, laser sensors utilize focused laser beams to provide highly accurate and rapid measurements of PM2.5 concentrations, making them ideal for industrial and environmental monitoring. Both types serve distinct needs within air quality management, contributing to improved public health and safety.</p></p>
