<p><p>The bio-based lysine market primarily comprises two types: crop raw materials and biofermentation processes. Crop raw materials involve the use of agricultural sources like corn and soybeans, which are rich in amino acids and serve as the foundation for lysine production. Conversely, biofermentation utilizes microbial fermentation techniques to convert sugar or starch into lysine with higher efficiency and sustainability. Both methods aim to produce lysine for animal feed, human nutrition, and other applications while reducing reliance on synthetic alternatives.</p></p>
