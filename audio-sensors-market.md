<p><p>The audio sensors market is primarily divided into two types: analog output and digital output. Analog output sensors produce continuous voltage or current signals that are proportional to sound levels, offering simplicity and compatibility with various devices. In contrast, digital output sensors convert sound into discrete signals, enabling higher accuracy and easier integration with digital systems. Both types cater to diverse applications, from consumer electronics to industrial monitoring, addressing the growing demand for advanced sound detection and processing technologies.</p></p>
