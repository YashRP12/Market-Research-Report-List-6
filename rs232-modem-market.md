<p><ul><li>Synchronize</li><li>Asynchronous</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The RS232 modem market is primarily divided into two types: synchronous and asynchronous. Synchronous modems transmit data in a continuous stream, requiring a clock signal for synchronization, making them suitable for high-speed data transfer in networked environments. In contrast, asynchronous modems send data in individual packets, using start and stop bits to manage communication, which is ideal for slower or less frequent transmissions. Both types serve distinct applications based on speed and data integrity requirements, catering to various industrial and commercial uses.</p></p>
