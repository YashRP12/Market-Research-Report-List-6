<p><p>The phenolic resin-based activated carbon fiber market is categorized into two primary types: chemical activation and physical activation methods. Chemical activation involves treating the phenolic resin with chemical agents at lower temperatures, enhancing pore development and surface area. In contrast, physical activation encompasses carbonization followed by oxidizing gas exposure, typically at high temperatures, to create porous structures. Both methods tailor the fiber properties for specific applications, such as adsorption, filtration, or catalysis, catering to diverse industrial needs.</p></p>
