<p><p>The refrigerator temperature sensors market comprises several types, each serving specific needs. Resistive type temperature sensors utilize resistance change in materials to measure temperature, ensuring accuracy. Infrared type sensors detect heat radiation from objects, allowing for non-contact temperature monitoring. Thermocouple type sensors work by generating a voltage difference in response to temperature variations, suited for extreme conditions. Other sensors include semiconductor devices and thermistors, broadening application scopes. Collectively, these sensors enhance energy efficiency and food preservation in refrigeration systems.</p></p>
