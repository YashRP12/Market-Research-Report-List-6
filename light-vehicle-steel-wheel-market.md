<p><p>The light vehicle steel wheel market encompasses three primary types: casting, forging, and other methods. Casting involves pouring molten steel into molds to create wheel shapes, allowing for complex designs and cost-effective production. Forging, on the other hand, involves shaping heated steel through compression, yielding stronger and lighter wheels. Other methods may include hybrid techniques or innovative manufacturing processes that combine various materials. Each type offers distinct advantages in terms of strength, weight, cost, and production efficiency, catering to diverse automotive requirements.</p></p>
