<p><p>The Network-Attached Storage (NAS) market is primarily divided into two types: On-Premise and Cloud-Based solutions. On-Premise NAS involves physical devices installed within an organization's infrastructure, providing direct control and low-latency access to data. In contrast, Cloud-Based NAS leverages remote servers managed by third-party providers, offering scalability, flexibility, and easy access from anywhere with an internet connection. Each type caters to different business needs, balancing factors such as cost, security, and accessibility for efficient data management.</p></p>
