<p><p>Front-end Medical Speech Recognition refers to real-time dictation where the healthcare provider directly speaks into a microphone and the spoken words are transcribed instantly into text. This technology helps in reducing documentation time and improving efficiency in healthcare settings. </p><p>On the other hand, Back-end Medical Speech Recognition involves the process of transcribing recorded dictations at a later time by a transcriptionist. This method allows for more accurate documentation as the transcriptions can be reviewed and edited before being finalized.</p></p>
