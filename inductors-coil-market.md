<p><ul><li>Fixed Inductance</li><li>Variable Inductance</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The inductor coil market is divided into two primary types: fixed inductance and variable inductance. Fixed inductance coils have a constant inductance value, ideal for applications requiring stability, such as power supplies and filters. In contrast, variable inductance coils allow for adjustable inductance levels, making them suitable for tuning circuits and applications that require flexibility, such as radio frequency devices. Both types play crucial roles in electronic circuits, supporting functions like energy storage, filtering, and signal processing.</p></p>
