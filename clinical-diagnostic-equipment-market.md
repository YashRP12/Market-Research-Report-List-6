<p><p>The clinical diagnostic equipment market comprises two primary types: handheld and benchtop devices. Handheld clinical diagnostic equipment is portable and designed for quick, point-of-care testing, allowing for immediate results in varied settings. Examples include glucose meters and hand-held electrocardiograms. In contrast, benchtop clinical diagnostic equipment is larger, typically designed for use in laboratories or medical facilities. These devices usually offer more comprehensive testing capabilities, such as analyzers for blood samples or chemical assays, ensuring accurate and detailed diagnostic results.</p></p>
