<p><p>Microsilica, also known as silica fume, is a byproduct of silicon and ferrosilicon production. It consists of very fine particles and is primarily used in construction and civil engineering applications due to its pozzolanic properties, enhancing the strength and durability of concrete. The market for microsilica has been experiencing robust growth, driven by increasing demand from the construction sector, especially in emerging economies. The need for high-performance concrete and environmentally friendly building materials is propelling this trend.</p><p>Recent trends in the microsilica market include a growing emphasis on sustainability, with more manufacturers focusing on recycling and utilizing industrial waste, thus reducing the carbon footprint. Additionally, innovations in microsilica formulations are enabling better performance in various applications, from high-strength concrete to refractory materials.</p><p>The increasing use of microsilica in advanced applications such as ultra-high-performance concrete, alongside the expansion of infrastructure projects worldwide, further supports market growth.
