<p><p>The emulsified asphalt market comprises three main types: anionic, cationic, and others. Anionic emulsified asphalt features negatively charged particles, making it suitable for use with alkaline aggregate surfaces and typically provides slower setting times. Cationic emulsified asphalt, on the other hand, contains positively charged particles, allowing for enhanced adhesion to various aggregates and quicker setting times. Other types may include specialized emulsions designed for specific applications or environments, addressing unique performance needs in construction and maintenance.</p></p>
