<p><p>Supercritical Fluid Chromatography (SFC) is an analytical technique that utilizes supercritical fluids, typically carbon dioxide, as the mobile phase for separating compounds in a mixture. This method combines features of both liquid and gas chromatography, providing advantages such as faster separation times, higher resolution, and reduced solvent consumption. SFC is widely used in pharmaceuticals, petrochemicals, and food industries for analyzing and purifying compounds.</p><p>The Supercritical Fluid Chromatography (SFC) Market is anticipated to grow at a CAGR of 8.6% during the forecast period, driven by the increasing demand for efficient analytical techniques in various industries. Moreover, the rise in environmental concerns encourages the shift from traditional organic solvents to greener alternatives such as supercritical fluids. The pharmaceutical industry is particularly influencing market growth due to the need for high-purity compounds and drug formulation studies. Innovations in stationary phases and the integration of SFC with mass spectrometry are also boosting its applicability, making it a preferred choice for many researchers and manufacturers.</p></p>
