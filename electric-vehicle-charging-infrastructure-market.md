<p><ul><li>Alternating Current (AC) Charger</li><li>Direct Current (DC) Charger</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The electric vehicle charging infrastructure market is primarily divided into two types: Alternating Current (AC) chargers and Direct Current (DC) chargers. AC chargers, typically used for residential and public charging stations, convert electricity from the grid to charge vehicles at slower rates, making them ideal for longer charging durations. In contrast, DC chargers deliver high-speed charging by directly supplying power to the battery, significantly reducing charging time. Together, they cater to diverse user needs and charging scenarios in the growing electric vehicle market.</p></p>
