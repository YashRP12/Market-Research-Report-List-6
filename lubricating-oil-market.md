<p><p>The lubricating oil market is categorized into four main types: liquid, solid, semi-liquid, and gaseous. Liquid lubricants are the most common, used widely in machinery and engines for reducing friction. Solid lubricants, such as graphite or molybdenum disulfide, are used in extreme conditions where liquid lubricants may fail. Semi-liquid lubricants, like greases, provide enhanced adhesion and protection. Gaseous lubricants, though less common, offer unique advantages in specific applications, providing low-friction conditions in certain industrial processes.</p></p>
