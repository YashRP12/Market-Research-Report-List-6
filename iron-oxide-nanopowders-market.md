<p><p>Iron oxide nanopowders are categorized based on particle size, influencing their applications and properties. Below 20nm nanoparticles exhibit unique magnetic and electronic characteristics, making them ideal for drug delivery and biosensing. Those sized 20-50nm offer improved stability and surface area, suitable for coatings and catalysis. Nanopowders in the 50-100nm range balance performance and viscosity, commonly utilized in electronics and pigments. Finally, particles above 100nm are often used in bulk applications like construction materials and agriculture, where finer attributes are less critical.</p></p>
