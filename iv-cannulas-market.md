<p><p>IV Cannulas are medical devices used to administer fluids, medication, and blood products into a patient's bloodstream. There are three main types of IV Cannulas on the market: Peripheral IV Cannula, Central Line IV Cannula, and Midline IV Cannula. Peripheral IV Cannulas are commonly used for short-term treatments and are inserted into the veins in the arms or hands. Central Line IV Cannulas are used for long-term treatments and are inserted into larger veins near the heart. Midline IV Cannulas are a middle-ground option, typically lasting longer than peripheral IVs but not as long as central lines.</p></p>
